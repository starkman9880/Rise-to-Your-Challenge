Watch how it works
The short version is that spaced repetition is how your brain is wired to encode memories. By seeing information again and again, you’re more likely to remember it.

BUT it's not that simple. To really leverage the efficiency of such a learning tool, the intervals between exposures need to be carefully calibrated according to how well you know the information. An you need to be honest with your self-assessments.

If you don’t know a concept well (and you rate it a 1 or a 2), you will be exposed to it more often. And if you do know a concept well (and rate it a 4 or 5), you will see it less often.
In this way, spaced repetition perpetually challenges you on your weakest areas of knowledge, while saving you time by phasing out review of the concepts you already know.
